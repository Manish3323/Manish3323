A picture, wound into a spiral

Inspired by 3Blue1Brown's video on the quiet geometry shared by design and math. Put a picture on the complex plane and two functions — the complex logarithm log(z) and its inverse exp(z) — turn ordinary shifts and rotations into spirals that converge to a point. Here is the whole trick, one step at a time.

All four panels run the same idea in a WebGL fragment shader: for each output pixel it walks the map backwards — plane → log → un-twist → strip — and reads the source colour there. The spiral you see is the shape of z → exp( m · log z ) for a complex number m; the twist slider is the argument of m, convergence is its magnitude. When m is tuned so the strip tiles seamlessly, this is exactly the classic Droste effect.
